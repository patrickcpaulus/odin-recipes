Sweet Potatoes

Description

This sweet potato recipe is an adapation of Basics With Babish's Thanskgiving Sides sweet potatoes dish, lightly modified from my experiences making the dish for my family. Unlike many classic sweet potato recipes, this one shies away from the marshamellows and heavy sugar use, and balances the natural sweetness from the potatoes with dried cranberries reconstituted in whiskey. The end result is a more complex flavor.

While Bulleit bourbon is the recommended whiskey, any whiskey will do. For additional flavor development, the potatoes and sugar mixture can be sealed in freezer bags and refrigerated several hours up to overnight to massicate. Conversely, store-bought ricotta can be used in lieu of the homemade stuff.

Ingredients

  • 3 large sweet potatoes, peeled and sliced into 1/2 inch pieces
  • 12 Tbsp butter
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup raw sugar (opt. 1/4 cup agave syrup)
  • 1 Tbsp cinnamon
  • 1-2 tsp freshly grated nutmeg
  • 1 cup bourbon
  • 1 cup craisins
  • 4-5 Tbsp ricotta (homemade, recipe to follow)
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, toasted

Steps

  1. Prepare sweet potatoes and set in a well-buttered casserole dish. Season with kosher salt.
  2. Combine butter, brown sugar, raw sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg in a small bowl, then pour over yams.
  3. Cover casserole dish and place in 375F oven for 30 minutes. Remove, mix, and return to the oven, uncovered, for 15-20 more minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, bring whiskey to a boil. Remove from heat, add craisins, and let sit covered for at least 15 minutes.
  5. Remove yams from the oven, stir, and transfer to a serving platter. Top with the ricotta, pecans, and whiskey-soaked craisins.
